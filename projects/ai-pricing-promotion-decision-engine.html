Applied AI Case Study

AI Pricing & Promotion Decision Engine

Built as a business-facing decision-support system, this project moves beyond pure forecasting by combining demand prediction, scenario simulation, and constrained action ranking for commercial teams.

Python pandas scikit-learn Streamlit forecasting simulation decision engine

Overview

Commercial pricing and promotion decisions involve competing objectives: volume uplift, revenue growth, and margin protection. The project was designed as an end-to-end analytics product that can evaluate options, enforce business constraints, and recommend actions in a structured, repeatable way.

Business Problem

Teams need to decide which products to promote and at what discount level, while demand is influenced by price elasticity, promotional effects, seasonality, stock constraints, and competitive context. Manual planning struggles to evaluate these tradeoffs consistently.

Approach

I designed a full decision workflow: generate realistic synthetic retail data, benchmark and tune demand prediction models, run counterfactual promotion scenarios, then rank recommended actions under explicit business constraints and weighted objectives.

Modeling and Validation

  • Built a synthetic dataset with realistic commercial dynamics, including elasticity, seasonality, and promo lift behavior.
  • Compared multiple regression model families with time-aware validation to preserve realistic forecasting conditions.
  • Selected and tuned models based on stable predictive behavior and business interpretability, not only on headline metrics.

Decision Engine Logic

  • Generated counterfactual scenarios for product-discount combinations to estimate demand and revenue outcomes.
  • Applied business constraints (inventory, discount bands, promotion limits) before recommendation ranking.
  • Used a weighted objective to rank actions by volume, revenue, and margin tradeoffs aligned to business priorities.

Dashboard / Product Interface

Results are surfaced in Streamlit through a business-facing interface where users can explore scenarios, review predicted outcomes, and compare recommended actions. The output design prioritizes decision usability over raw model diagnostics.

Why This Project Matters

This project demonstrates applied Data Science beyond model training: translating an ambiguous commercial problem into a structured decision system that combines forecasting, simulation, optimization logic, and productized analytics delivery.

Tech Stack

Python, pandas, scikit-learn, Streamlit, time-aware validation, scenario simulation, and constrained recommendation logic.
