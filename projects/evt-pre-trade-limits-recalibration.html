Real-Time Pre-Trade Limits Recalibration using EVT

Built during my thesis at Societe Generale ATS, this project replaces manually tuned pre-trade thresholds with a statistically grounded Extreme Value Theory (POT/GPD) framework — calibrated on 12M+ trades, packaged with full MLOps infrastructure, and validated against real business constraints around false positives and regulatory capital.

Python EVT / POT GPD SQL scikit-learn Docker GitHub Actions MLOps

Business Problem

Pre-trade limit controls are a core component of trading risk management. They block or flag orders that breach predefined thresholds before they reach the market. The challenge: these thresholds were historically calibrated by hand, using heuristic rules based on averages and static percentiles.

This manual approach created a fundamental tension. Thresholds set too conservatively generated excessive false alerts — operational noise that desensitized teams and added friction. Thresholds set too loosely missed genuine risk events. Neither outcome was acceptable in a high-volume, regulatory-sensitive trading environment processing millions of trades.

The business needed a data-driven recalibration methodology: one that could estimate the true statistical behavior of extreme trade events, propose calibrated thresholds with quantified confidence, and produce outputs usable by both risk teams and regulators.

Technical Approach

1. Data Engineering & Feature Pipeline

Ingested and reconciled 12M+ trade records via SQL pipelines. Built feature engineering logic for trade-level signals: notional size, time-of-day patterns, instrument volatility, and historical breach rates. Applied outlier handling and reproducible preprocessing to ensure consistent inputs to the EVT modeling layer.

2. Extreme Value Theory — Peak-Over-Threshold

Used the Peak-Over-Threshold (POT) method from Extreme Value Theory to model the tail distribution of trade notionals. Fitted Generalized Pareto Distributions (GPD) to exceedances above a calibrated threshold, providing statistically justified estimates of extreme quantiles — the foundation for proposed limit values. Selected POT over block maxima for better sample efficiency on the full trade population.

3. Threshold Proposal & Backtesting

The EVT model outputs proposed threshold candidates at configurable confidence levels. These were backtested against historical data and compared to the current heuristic rules across multiple periods and instrument segments. Evaluation metrics were framed around operational outcomes: false-positive rate, missed breach rate, and sensitivity across volatility regimes.

4. MLOps Packaging & Deployment

Packaged the full pipeline with unit tests, Docker containerization, GitHub Actions for CI, and monitoring hooks for threshold drift detection. The deployment layer produced versioned threshold proposals with metadata — making outputs auditable, reproducible, and ready for operational review by risk and compliance teams.

What I Learned & What I'd Improve

Key Learnings

  • — EVT's POT method is well-suited for financial control problems but requires careful threshold selection (the exceedance threshold, not the proposed limit) — stability plots and mean excess plots are essential diagnostics.
  • — Business validation mattered as much as statistical validation: a 94% precise model is only useful if risk teams trust and understand the proposed thresholds.
  • — MLOps packaging from day one (tests, Docker, CI) made the difference between a research prototype and an operationally usable tool.

What I'd Improve Next

  • — Segment the EVT model by instrument type and market regime — a single global model likely smooths over meaningful tail differences across equity, rate, and credit products.
  • — Add an online recalibration loop to detect threshold drift in real time, rather than relying on scheduled refit cycles.
  • — Build an explainability layer for business stakeholders — showing which trades drove threshold shifts and why, not just the proposed value.
